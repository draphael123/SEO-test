Frequently Asked Questions

What is Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)?
TRT is a medical treatment aimed at restoring testosterone levels in men experiencing low testosterone (hypogonadism). It can help address symptoms like fatigue, low libido, mood changes, and reduced muscle mass when clinically appropriate. We offer flexible delivery methods including injections and topical cream, allowing you and your clinician to choose the option that best fits your lifestyle and preferences.
What is Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT)?
HRT involves supplementing hormones like estrogen and progesterone to alleviate symptoms associated with menopause and hormonal imbalances in women. It's designed to help manage symptoms like hot flashes, night sweats, mood changes, and sleep issues.
How do I know if TRT or HRT is right for me?
A clinician considers symptoms, medical history, and (when relevant) labs together to determine whether treatment is appropriate. Common indicators include persistent symptoms that affect quality of life, lab results showing hormone deficiencies, and ruling out other causes.
Do I need labs?
Many care plans involve labs for evaluation and/or ongoing monitoring. What's needed depends on your situation and clinician judgment. Labs help establish baseline levels, monitor safety, and guide treatment adjustments.
How does the treatment process work?
After an initial online intake and consultation, a clinician reviews your information and may order labs. Once approved, a personalized treatment plan is developed. Medications are delivered to your doorstep, and ongoing support and monitoring are provided through follow-up consultations.
How does follow-up and monitoring work?
Follow-ups review progress, symptoms, and (when appropriate) lab markers so a clinician can make careful adjustments and support safety. Regular check-ins help ensure optimal dosing and address any concerns.
What delivery methods are available for TRT?
We offer two delivery methods for TRT patients: injections and topical cream. Your clinician will help determine which option is best for you based on your preferences, lifestyle, and medical considerations. Injections are typically administered weekly or bi-weekly, while topical cream is applied daily. Both methods are effective, and the choice depends on what works best for your individual situation.
Are there any side effects?
As with any medical treatment, there can be side effects. Common ones may include injection site reactions (for injections), skin irritation at the application site (for cream), mood changes, or other effects depending on the delivery method. It's essential to discuss potential risks with your healthcare provider and report any concerns promptly.
Is everything "all-inclusive"?
Offerings vary by plan and by state law. This page is a general overview; specifics are confirmed during intake and clinician review. Most plans include medication, consultations, and monitoring, but exact inclusions depend on your location and selected program.

Can you guarantee outcomes?
No. Responses vary by person. Any medical decision should be individualized and guided by a licensed clinician. Results depend on many factors including adherence to treatment, individual physiology, and underlying health conditions.
